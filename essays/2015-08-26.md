Software engineering is typically defined as "taking a systematic engineering approach to the maintenance and development of software." When I think of software engineering, I think of it as a necessary skill to have in order for a computer scientist to be successful in their field and not just a mandatory class that I inevitably had to take to get my B.S. in Computer Science. The reason why I think it is so important to have is because of its seemingly endless applications and diversity. Especially in a world dominated by technology and a time plagued by a pandemic, being a computer scientist with software engineering skills is now more urgent than ever. These reasons alone are enough for me to have an interest and motivation to study software engineering. However, growing up surrounded by technology and becoming savvy with it also has some influence over this interest as well as the realization that my future will eventually become even more dominated by technology. And so I thought that it would be to my benefit that I obtain a degree in Computer Science. I hope that by going down this career path and making reasonable professional decisions, I develop invaluable skills not only in software engineering or computer science but in practical settings such as leadership, project management, and collaboration.
